Competitive Analysis

How do users decide which game, film, book, or show to immerse themselves in?

With this in mind, I chose three different companies that focus on different aspects in the entertainment industry. In this analysis I used:

  • Steam - largest digital distribution platform for PC gaming
  • Goodreads - well known social catalog website for books
  • HBO Go - streaming service for films and shows

Ideation & Sketching

Planning Interface Layout

After understanding the product fully and analyzing the competitors, I made several lists. The first two "User Feedback" and "Problems" are my Keep in Mind lists. They are made so that I stay consistent with the goals I’m trying to achieve when redesigning the app interface. The last list "Top Features," includes new functionalities that I would like to incorporate.

User Feedback
Based off of the user feedback stated in the brief, I ranked the problems in order of highest to lowest priority.

Problems
From accessing the original application, I noticed two additional issues:

Users
The first page of the story catalog will always be the same. This will result in users seeing the same stories every time they open the app.

Authors
Since the stories are created by multiple authors, how can I make it so that current and new authors are noticed and encouraged to submit additional stories.

Top Features
I decided to come up with new features that will incorporate the users suggestions along with improving the current interface of the app.

Addressing User Feedback

A way to pick a story based on the topic.

Updating Story Cards

The issue was taking too long in finding a bedtime story to read together with their child. My solution was to improve the design of the story cards to emphasize what the story will entail.

Issue of selecting an appropriate story that was comprehensible to their child

Include an ndication of the time it takes to finish a story

Filter Options

The second and third most problem that users ran into was the lack of a personalized experience. Creating a filtering method to limit the amount of stories shown was a way of fixing this.

  • Age: a drop-down menu displaying 5 different age groups.
  • Length: four groups of time lengths ranging from less then 15 minutes to greater than one hour.
  • Tag: an additional option to add and search tags to narrow down results further.

Parents would like to share specific bedtime favorites with their friends.

Out of all the feedback that was given, knowing about the author was the least of their priorities, but should still be included to recognize the story contributors.

Creating the Story Details

With recommendation and author coming in 4th and 5th most importance. I decided that these two types of information would overcrowd the story cards and needed their own section. Designing detailed page of the story was one way of understanding how a story feels and if it's the right one.

  • Age: a drop-down menu displaying 5 different age groups.
  • Length: four groups of time lengths ranging from less then 15 minutes to greater than one hour.
  • Tag: an additional option to add and search tags to narrow down results further.

Creating New Features

With the storycard finished, parents wanted a way to discover new stories. Instead of a story catalog that was static like in the original app, how about an explore page?

Explore

A page specifically designed to discover and try out new stories.

  • Categories such as newly added, most recommended, most reviewed
  • Spotlight feature to highlight new authors and browse through collections of bundled stories

With all the main problems solved that current users faced. I thought about the process of reading and I wondered where are those stories being stored? How can users go back to their favorite stories, or continuing reading?

Shelf

A place for accessing your favorites, in-progress, read later, and recommended stories.
