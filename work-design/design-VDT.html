PROCESS

Understand & Discover

Stakeholders

To further understand how theatre production worked, we discussed the people involved. Whether they will be directly or indirectly affected by our system, we made sure to include them.

Personas

To focus on a human centered design approach, we narrowed down our target audience to three main roles in the theatre business. This included:

  • Director
  • Scenic Designer
  • Production Manager

Use Cases

After understanding each personaâ€™s goals, frustrations, and technology abilities we moved on to creating use cases of the first two personas Jonathan Graham (Director) and Rachel Moore (Scenic Designer).

Collaborting with Developer

The client had an idea of how the application would function. However, he wanted the developer to get started in building a small working prototype that will be shared to other stakeholders. In this case, I decided to use InVision Freehand to collaborate with the developer and figure out the core functions of the VR program.

User Flow

We knew who to design the system for, but what exact tools did they need for a typical work day? What tasks did scenic designers and directors need to to achieve their goals? In addition, how can we optimize their current work flow process and keep consideration of their technical abilities. These were questions we discussed to help in figuring out the main functionalities of the program.

I decided to use the tool Figma to create low-fidelity wireframes. First I focused on one persona, gave them a task, and drew up wireframes that followed their process if they were to use the application. I worked with the client weekly and used Figma as a collaboration tool to figure out how the users would interact with the application.

After a few weeks of discussing the different personas, we came to the conclusion of our final flow. I decided that we should dive in further to discuss the details with the developer and make any changes needed when walking through the process.

Competitor Analysis

It took a bit of time to figure out our final MVP. However, I wanted to make sure that we were on the right track and look into our competition. Where exactly does our product stand out? Are there any opportunities or threats that we should take in consideration? How can we be progressive with this application to avoid copying other products?
