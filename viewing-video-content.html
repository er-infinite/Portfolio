VIEWING VIDEO CONTENT

QUALITATIVE RESEARCH

Introduction

As technology improves everyday, individuals are utalizing different devices in order to view video content. Whether it is a show, film, music video, or short clip, understanding a user's perspective will give additional insight on the topic. Our goal through this project, will help in determining the current stance of technology that is capable of viewing video content and the future of it.

Research Topic & Questions

How Do People Watch Video Content?

  • What is your preferred device to watch videos on?
  • How open are you to new technologies, like virtual reality?

Research Paper

Full details to research project can be found here.

PROCESS

METHOD

User Interviews

Steps

  1. Recruit two participants
  2. Read over interview protocol
  3. Interview participants with interview protocol while also recording the audio
  4. Transcribe interview
  5. Analyze data

Participant Criteria

Every student in the class was asked to interview two people. They can be a friend, stranger, or a family member. However, it was required to conduct the one to one interview in person, through phone, or video.

  • One individual under 50 years old
  • One individual above 50 years old

Parts of Interview Protocol

  1. Getting to know you questions
  2. Basic behavior questions related to research question
  3. In-depth Questions

As a class we made a interview protocol containing questions pertaining to how they view video content, favorite forms of video, time spent watching, if whether or not they watch with other people, and other similar questions. Our aim was to conduct interviews for a minimum of 30 minutes.

Participants

As mentioned before, for this assignment we were only required to randomly select 20 participants. 10 under the age of 50 and 10 above the age of 50.

RESULTS

Themes

After reading through the 20 transcripts, I organized the data in a Excel sheet. Based off the two research questions I mentioned earlier, I came up with two themes.

Current Existing Technology Capable of Viewing Video Content

Research Question: What is your preferred device to watch videos on?

Televisions were invented in the late 1920s, however the television broadcasting that we know of today pre-dates to 1936. As of now, multiple devices used by millions are being innovated today and are far superior then current existing television systems.

Preferred Viewing Device

Above 50 Years Old

  • 10% Laptop
  • 10% Phone
  • 70% TV
  • 10% Tablet

Below 50 Years Old

  • 30% Laptop
  • 50% Phone
  • 20% TV
  • 0% Tablet

Above 50 Years Old

Those who were older than 50 they mainly faced problems due to their eyesight, lack of knowledge of how to use current technologies, and overall preferred bigger screens.

DAY 2

Creating Solutions

For this portion, we developed our own solutions individually. We followed the four-step sketch which involves note taking, jotting down ideas, drawing up crazy 8s, and finally coming up with the solution sketch.

Everyone shared an application or website that they will draw their inspiration from. I decided to focus on developing a way to understand the sense of a club or organization.

In this case, I referred to Spotify, a music streaming service. Contained inside are thousands of artists. I wondered how did users decide which one to listen to?

DAY 3

Critiques

Next step was showcasing our sketches and voting for the ones that closely relates to our original challenge: How to improve student experiences by focusing on student social life at New Jersey Institute of Technology.

Voting

Everyone was given strips of post-it notes to cast their votes. As Decider, I was in charge of the supervote.

I voted based on sketches that followed our long-term goal and had a good foundation. This will ensure that it'll be a sustainable app that can be reused. We then separated the winners from the losers. Any ideas or concepts on the winners side will be used in our app.

Storyboarding

In light of the user, we created a storyboard to show how a typical day will look if they used our app. We grabbed post-its from the winners side to help build the application we had in mind.
